## ServerSpace Introduction

ServerSpace is an international cloud provider that's been quietly doing its thing since they set up shop. They're part of the ITGLOBAL.COM Group, which means they've got serious infrastructure backing them up. What makes them different? They deploy your virtual servers in about 40 seconds. Not "up to" 40 seconds, not "eventually" – just done.

The company operates data centers across seven locations: New Jersey and Toronto in North America, Amsterdam in Europe, São Paulo in Brazil, Almaty in Kazakhstan, Istanbul in Turkey, and Dubai in the UAE. That's actual global coverage, not just saying they're "worldwide" while having two data centers in the same country.

Their infrastructure runs on Intel Xeon Gold 6254 processors (3.1 GHz, 18 cores) with DDR4 ECC RAM and NVMe SSDs. They use a vStack hyperconverged platform with the bhyve hypervisor. For the non-technical folks, that means your server runs fast and doesn't randomly die when hardware fails because everything's redundant.

What's interesting about ServerSpace is their billing model. They charge every 10 minutes. Not monthly, not hourly – every 10 minutes. So if you spin up a server for testing and shut it down after 30 minutes, you pay for 30 minutes. This pay-as-you-go approach actually makes sense when you're not locked into monthly commitments you don't need.

They offer Linux VPS, Windows VPS, VMware cloud, Kubernetes, private networks, CDN, and a bunch of other services. But the real story is how they've made everything accessible through a Single Page Application control panel that loads instantly after the first page. No waiting around for pages to load while you're trying to manage servers.
